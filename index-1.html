About our solar system

  • Includes The Sun
  • Bodies

About Sun :

    Type:

  • Yellow star contains 99.86% of the solar system mass.

    • Key Role:

  • Provides light, heat, and gravitational stability.

    • Layers:

  • Core (fusion powerhouse), photosphere, chromosphere, and corona.

    • Solar Activity:

  • Sunspots, solar flares, and coronal mass ejections (CMEs).

THE NAMES OF PLANETS IN SOLAR SYSTEM :


Mercury :

    Closest planet to the Sun :

  • smallest in the solar system (slightly larger than Earth’s Moon).

    • Surface :

  • Cratered, rocky, with extreme temperature swings (-180°C to 430°C).

    • No atmosphere :

  • Only a thin exosphere blasted by solar wind.

    • Core :

  • Massive iron core (~85% of its radius), creating a weak magnetic field.

    • Ice :

  • Water ice hides in shadowed polar craters.
    • Fun Fact : A day on Mercury (sunrise to sunrise) lasts 176 Earth days!

VENUS :

    Hottest planet :

  • (465°C/870°F); Earth’s "twin" in size.

    • Atmosphere :

  • Thick CO₂ clouds + sulfuric acid rain.

    • Surface:

  • Volcanic, 90% basalt plains; crushing pressure (92x Earth’s).

    • Spin :

  • Retrograde (backward) + day longer than year (243 vs. 225 Earth days).

    • Explored :

  • Venera (USSR), Magellan (NASA), Akatsuki (JAXA).

EARTH :

    Atmosphere :

  • 3rd from the Sun.

    • Surface :

  • 78% N₂, 21% O₂ + protective ozone layer.

    • Surface :

  • 71% water (oceans), active tectonics, diverse biosphere.

    • Moon :

  • Stabilizes tilt + tides.

    • Human Impact :

  • Climate change, pollution, biodiversity loss.

    • Explored :

  • Climate change, pollution, biodiversity loss.

    • Fun Fact:

  • Earth’s rotation slows ~1.8 ms/century (Moon’s tidal pull)!

Mars :

    Red Planet :

  • (iron oxide dust); 4th from the Sun.

    • Atmosphere :

  • Thin CO₂; -63°C avg; dust storms engulf the planet.

    • Surface :

  • Olympus Mons (tallest volcano), Valles Marineris (vast canyon), ancient riverbeds.

    • Water :

  • Ice at poles + likely subsurface; past lakes/oceans.

    • Missions :

  • Rovers (Perseverance, Curiosity), helicopter (Ingenuity), plans for human colonization.

    • Moons :

  • Phobos + Deimos (captured asteroids).

    • Fun Fact:

  • A Mars day (sol) is ~24h 37m – closest to Earth’s!

Jupter :

    Largest planet :

  • (11x Earth’s width); gas giant (hydrogen/helium).

    • Great Red Spot :

  • Giant storm (400+ years old; shrinking).

    • Moons :

  • 95+ (Galilean moons: Europa, Ganymede, Io, Callisto).

    • Rings :

  • Faint, dusty (discovered 1979).

    • Magnetic field :

  • Strongest in solar system (radiation belts).

    • Explored :

  • Voyagers, Galileo, Juno (active orbiter since 2016).

    • Fun Fact:

  • Jupiter’s mass is 2.5x all other planets combined – a “failed star”!

SATURN :

    6th planet; gas giant :

  • (hydrogen/helium) with iconic ice/rock rings.

    • Rings :

  • Billions of chunks, 280,000 km wide (faint gaps like Cassini Division

    • Moons :

  • 146+ (Titan has lakes of methane; Enceladus spews ice geysers).

    • Density :

  • 146+ (Titan has lakes of methane; Enceladus spews ice geysers).

    • Explored :

  • Less than water (would float in a giant bathtub!).

    • Weather :

  • Voyagers, Cassini-Huygens (2004–2017), Dragonfly (future Titan drone).

    • Fun Fact:

  • Saturn’s rings are young (~100 million years old; dinosaurs saw them form)!

URANUS :

    Ice giant :

  • Methane/ammonia/water ices. 7th planet

    • Atmosphere :

  • Blue-green (methane), -224°C.

    • Explored: :

  • Only Voyager 2 (1986).

    • Year :

  • 84 Earth years (21-year seasons!).

NEPTUNE :

    8th planet; ice giant :

  • (methane/ammonia/water ices).

    • Atmosphere :

  • Blue (methane), supersonic winds (2,100 km/h).

    • Moons :

  • 14+ (Triton has geysers; orbits backward).

    • Rings :

  • Faint, clumpy (discovered 1984).

    • Explored :

  • Only Voyager 2 (1989 flyby).

    • Discovery :

  • Predicted by math before telescopes spotted it (1846).

    • Fun Fact :

  • Triton is slowly spiraling toward Neptune – will shatter into rings in ~100 million years!
